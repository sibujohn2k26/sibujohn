Gas Law

Gas Volume at STP

Moles (n) = Volume (V) ÷ Molar Volume (22.4 L)

1 Mol Helium
4.0 g
Volume = 22.4 L
1 Mol Oxygen
32.0 g
Volume = 22.4 L

Avogadro's Hypothesis: Even though Oxygen molecules are 8 times heavier than Helium atoms, 1 mole of both occupy the exact same volume (22.4 L) at STP because gas molecules are far apart!
